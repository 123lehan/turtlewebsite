Bentota Hatchery


The Bentota Turtle Hatchery is a well-known conservation project located in Bentota, Sri Lanka. It is dedicated to the protection and preservation of sea turtles and their eggs. The hatchery aims to increase the survival rates of sea turtle hatchlings and contribute to the overall conservation efforts for these endangered creatures. At the hatchery, visitors can learn about the different species of sea turtles found in Sri Lanka, including the Olive Ridley, Green, Hawksbill, Loggerhead, and Leatherback turtles. The knowledgeable staff at the hatchery provide educational tours and share information about the turtles' life cycles, threats they face, and the conservation measures being taken. One of the main activities at the Bentota Turtle Hatchery is the collection of sea turtle eggs from vulnerable nesting sites along the coast. The eggs are then brought to the hatchery for incubation in a protected environment. This helps to safeguard the eggs from natural predators and human interference. Once the eggs hatch, the baby turtles are released into the ocean, giving them a better chance of survival. The hatchery also provides medical care and rehabilitation for injured or sick turtles. They have tanks where turtles with injuries or disabilities are nurtured back to health before being released into the sea. The facility plays a crucial role in raising awareness about the importance of sea turtle conservation and the need to protect their natural habitats. Visiting the Bentota Turtle Hatchery allows you to observe these fascinating creatures up close and learn about the conservation efforts being undertaken to ensure their survival. It provides a unique opportunity to support the cause of sea turtle preservation and contribute to the conservation of these magnificent marine animals.

  • Location: The Bentota Turtle Hatchery is located in the town of Induruwa, which is approximately 65 kilometers (40 miles) south of Colombo, the capital city of Sri Lanka. It is situated along the southwestern coast of the country.
  • Conservation Activities: The hatchery plays a vital role in sea turtle conservation through several activities: Egg Collection: The hatchery staff patrols the beaches during nesting season to identify and relocate sea turtle nests to safer areas within the hatchery premises. This protects the eggs from natural threats, such as predators or human disturbances. Incubation: The collected eggs are carefully placed in sand-filled incubation chambers where temperature and humidity conditions are controlled. This helps simulate the natural nesting environment, increasing the chances of successful hatching. Release of Hatchlings: Once the eggs hatch, usually after a period of around 45-60 days, the baby turtles are carefully released into the sea during nighttime. This minimizes their exposure to potential predators and increases their survival rate. Rehabilitation: The Bentota Turtle Hatchery also provides medical care and rehabilitation for injured or sick turtles. They have facilities to treat turtles affected by injuries caused by fishing nets, boat propellers, or pollution. The turtles are nursed back to health before being released back into their natural habitat.
  • Education and Awareness: The hatchery aims to raise awareness among visitors, particularly tourists, about the importance of sea turtle conservation. They conduct educational tours and provide information about the different turtle species, their life cycles, and the threats they face. By educating the public, the hatchery hopes to foster a sense of responsibility and encourage conservation efforts.
  • Visitor Experience: Visitors to the Bentota Turtle Hatchery can observe the turtles at various stages of their life cycle, from eggs to hatchlings to fully grown adults. The facility provides a hands-on experience where visitors can hold and interact with baby turtles, promoting a deeper understanding and connection with these remarkable creatures. There is usually an entrance fee to support the conservation efforts of the hatchery.
  • Local Community Engagement: The Bentota Turtle Hatchery actively involves the local community in its conservation efforts. They work closely with fishermen, educating them about sustainable fishing practices that minimize harm to sea turtles. Additionally, the hatchery provides employment opportunities to local residents, creating a positive impact on the local economy.

The Bentota Turtle Hatchery Project is a popular attraction in Bentota for all travellers. The pristine golden shores that draws you to our island, also provides an enticing nesting ground for several endangered turtle species from the Olive Ridley to the Leatherback turtle. The project aims at conserving and protecting the eggs from predators and other dangers. Visit the turtle hatchery in Bentota to learn about these endangered graceful creatures of the sea.

Overall, the Bentota Turtle Hatchery serves as an important conservation hub for sea turtles in Sri Lanka. It combines education, research, and rehabilitation to protect these endangered species and contribute to their long-term survival.

n conclusion, the Bentota Turtle Hatchery in Sri Lanka is a significant conservation project dedicated to the protection and preservation of sea turtles. The hatchery plays a crucial role in increasing the survival rates of sea turtle hatchlings by collecting and incubating their eggs in a safe environment. It also provides medical care and rehabilitation for injured or sick turtles, promoting their recovery before releasing them back into the wild. The Bentota Turtle Hatchery not only focuses on conservation efforts but also aims to raise awareness among visitors about the importance of sea turtle preservation. Through educational tours and interactive experiences, visitors can learn about different turtle species, their life cycles, and the challenges they face. By engaging the local community and promoting sustainable fishing practices, the hatchery contributes to the long-term conservation of sea turtles. Overall, the Bentota Turtle Hatchery serves as a valuable resource for research, education, and conservation, working towards the goal of protecting and ensuring the survival of endangered sea turtles. It offers visitors a unique opportunity to support the cause of sea turtle preservation and witness these magnificent creatures up close.
